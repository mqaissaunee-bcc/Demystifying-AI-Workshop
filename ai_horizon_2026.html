01. The Research

The 2026 Shift: From Experimentation to Integration

The "Wild West" era is over. Institution-wide AI adoption in higher education jumped from 49% in 2024 to 66% in 2025 — and 86% of students globally now use AI in their studies. This is the era of teacher-in-the-loop workflows, embedded ed-AI, and hyper-personalized scalability.

Equity & Scalability

AI-powered adaptive engines identify student misconceptions in real time, providing 1-to-1 instructional support that was previously impossible at scale.

Ellucian State of AI in Higher Education, 2025

Teacher De-burdening

Teachers using AI weekly save an average of 5.9 hours per week — equivalent to six full weeks per school year — reclaimed for mentorship, feedback, and student relationships.

Gallup & Walton Family Foundation, 2025

Agentic Futures

We are moving from chat-based AI toward agentic AI — systems that execute multi-step projects, from organizing assignments to mapping long-term degree paths.

WEF Future of Jobs, 2025
Why this matters: 92% of UK undergraduates now report using AI tools in their studies (HEPI 2025), up from 66% the year prior. Faculty cannot opt out; the question is whether to design intentionally or react after the fact.

02. Student Literacy

The 4 C's Framework

AI literacy is no longer about learning to use a tool — it is foundational competency for the 2030 workforce. Explore the four pillars adapted from the SchoolAI 4 C's framework for higher-ed contexts.

Conscientious Use

Empowering students to understand their digital footprint, the environmental impact of high-compute models, data privacy and ownership, and the ethical implications of AI-augmented work.

Classroom Application

"The Data Trail" — students map the likely origin of data behind a specific AI output to understand training bias, ownership, and limitations.

03. Future Outlook

The Workforce of 2030

The 2025 WEF Future of Jobs Report projects 39% of core worker skills will change by 2030, with 170 million new roles created and 92 million displaced — a net gain of 78 million jobs. We aren't training for jobs that exist today, but for a "human premium" economy.

Declining Relevance

  • Routine data entry and processing
  • Basic syntax and code-writing tasks
  • Standard data cleaning and formatting
  • Initial content drafting from a brief
  • Routine language translation
  • Administrative scheduling and intake
Source: WEF Future of Jobs Report, 2025

The Human Premium

  • AI & big data fluency
  • Networks & cybersecurity literacy
  • Complex ethical judgment
  • Creative thinking & resilience
  • Cross-disciplinary synthesis
  • AI systems orchestration
