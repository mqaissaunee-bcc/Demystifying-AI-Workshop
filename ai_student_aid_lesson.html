AI & Your Financial Aid:
What Students Need to Know

Why This Matters

A Headline That Affects You Directly

In early March 2026, a legal group that works to protect students' rights announced it was investigating whether the federal government is using artificial intelligence (AI) to manage student financial aid — possibly without proper safeguards or oversight.

This matters to you because federal student aid — things like Pell Grants and federal student loans — helps millions of community college students afford college. If AI is making decisions about your financial aid, you deserve to know how it works, whether it's accurate, and who is responsible when it makes a mistake.

Article Summary

What the News Article Says

Inside Higher Ed — March 6, 2026

A student advocacy organization called Student Defense announced it is investigating how the Trump administration is using AI to run federal student aid programs. The group is worried that AI tools are being used without proper training for staff or protections for student data privacy.

The federal government sent a memo in early 2025 telling all agencies to "speed up" their use of AI. In response, Student Defense filed 12 public records requests — formal asks for government documents — to find out what AI is doing in areas like: answering student questions and complaints, creating rules for loan forgiveness and repayment plans, and processing veterans' education benefits.

The group's president said, "The American people deserve to know what's being outsourced to AI tools, whether they can rely on the information they're getting from the government, and whether their data is being protected."

Explore the Ideas

3 Key Ideas to Understand

Select each idea below to read a short explanation and guided questions to consider.

Governments have traditionally used human employees to process aid applications, answer student questions, and write regulations (rules about how programs work). Now, AI systems — programs that can analyze large amounts of information and generate responses — are being used to do some of that work.

A 2025 White House memo told every federal agency to accelerate the use of AI. This means the Federal Student Aid office may be using AI to respond to student complaints, draft new regulations for programs like Public Service Loan Forgiveness, or process veterans' benefits — without the public fully knowing about it.

💬 Guided Questions

  1. If you called your financial aid office and received a response written by an AI instead of a person, would you know the difference? Does it matter who — or what — answered you?
  2. What are one or two benefits of using AI to handle student aid questions? What could go wrong?
  3. Should the government be required to tell students when AI is making decisions about their financial aid? Why or why not?

When you apply for federal student aid using the FAFSA, you give the government very sensitive information: your Social Security Number, your family's income and tax records, and details about your household. This data is meant to be protected by federal privacy laws.

Student Defense is concerned that when the government uses AI tools built by private technology companies, that sensitive data could be shared with those companies — possibly without the legal protections students are promised. If those companies do not follow federal privacy laws, students could be harmed by data breaches or misuse of their personal information.

💬 Guided Questions

  1. What kinds of personal information does your FAFSA include? List as many as you can think of. Why could that information be dangerous in the wrong hands?
  2. Do you think students should be asked for their consent (permission) before their data is used by AI systems? How might that work in practice?
  3. Who should be responsible if an AI system mishandles a student's private data — the government, the tech company, or both?

Oversight means having systems in place to check that a program is working correctly and fairly. Accountability means someone is responsible when things go wrong.

Student Defense is using FOIA requests (Freedom of Information Act requests) to force the government to release documents about its AI use. They are also working with members of Congress and plan to open a tip line so government employees can report problems. Their argument is that when AI systems make consequential decisions — like whether your loan is forgiven or how your complaint is resolved — there must be transparency (openness), trained staff, and ways to appeal or correct errors.

💬 Guided Questions

  1. Can you think of a situation where an AI error in the financial aid process could seriously affect a student's life? What would the student's options be?
  2. FOIA requests are a tool for government transparency. Can you name any other ways citizens can hold the government accountable?
  3. If you were designing an AI system to handle student aid, what rules or safeguards would you build in to protect students?
